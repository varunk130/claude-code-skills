# Game Theory Skills

Apply game theory to real decisions: pricing wars, negotiation, mechanism design, repeated interactions, and competitive dynamics. Built on Nash, Vickrey, Schelling, Axelrod, Myerson, and the modern IO literature.

| Skill | What it produces |
|-------|------------------|
| [competitive-response-modeler](competitive-response-modeler/SKILL.md) | Multi-round action-reaction tree with reaction functions, commitment moves, and signaling analysis |
| [mechanism-design-planner](mechanism-design-planner/SKILL.md) | Mechanism design for auctions, matching, and pricing with IC, IR, revenue equivalence, and collusion stress tests |
| [nash-equilibrium-solver](nash-equilibrium-solver/SKILL.md) | Pure and mixed Nash equilibria, dominance, subgame perfection, and equilibrium selection |
| [negotiation-strategist](negotiation-strategist/SKILL.md) | BATNA, ZOPA, multi-issue trade matrix, anchoring plan, concession schedule, tactical playbook |
| [repeated-game-strategist](repeated-game-strategist/SKILL.md) | Critical discount factor, strategy selection (TFT, grim, Pavlov), reputation, and retaliation policy |

## Who these are for

Executives, founders, PMs, dealmakers, procurement leads, and strategists making decisions where the outcome depends on what other strategic actors do.

## Design principles

- **Formalize before solving** — players, actions, information, payoffs explicit
- **Match the equilibrium concept to the situation** — one-shot vs. repeated, complete vs. incomplete information
- **Predict the response, then the response to the response** — terminal equilibria matter, not round-zero
- **Stress-test for noise and gaming** — every recommendation includes robustness checks
- **Translate theory into action** — each output ends in a concrete move
